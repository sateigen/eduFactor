About eduFactor | Comfort or Panic?

Interactive tests are fantastic, allowing for virtually unlimited question design without endless sheets of paper, but changing the testing environment can be difficult for our students. eduFactor aims to help reduce test anxiety for elementary-aged students with our friendly, step-by-step walkthroughs of the math questions they are actually facing on their standardized tests. Students point to high levels of test anxiety as a main cause of poor performance (Cassady, Johnson 2001, p.288). Thankfully, education research has found that using web-based, testing practice has a significant impact on scoring (Martindale, et. al 2005, p.356). eduFactor takes the web-based practicing one step further with our detailed tutorials that help students break down each question. Familiarizing the student with the testing environment helps lower their anxiety (Hembree 1998, p.67). Studies have shown the most effective techniques for reducing anxiety involve focusing on the problem, while remaining relatively brief (Ergene 2003, p.326). eduFactor uses quick, friendly interactions to reinforce strong test-taking habits while taking the stigma away from testing.
